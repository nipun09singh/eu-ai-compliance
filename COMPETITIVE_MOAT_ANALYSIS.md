## 6. Threats & Risks

### HIGH Risk
1. **Vanta adds EU AI Act module** — They already serve SMBs for SOC 2/ISO 27001 and have PLG distribution. If they add EU AI Act, they have instant channel.
   - *Mitigation:* Ship before them, build domain expertise brand, offer deeper EU AI Act coverage than a module bolted onto a generic platform.

2. **trail-ml launches self-serve tier** — They're the closest competitor with existing EU AI Act classification. Adding a €99 self-serve tier is trivial for them.
   - *Mitigation:* They're German enterprise-DNA. Going self-serve requires different GTM, different support model, different pricing infrastructure. It's possible but unlikely in <6 months.

3. **Regulation changes or delayed enforcement** — If EU extends the Aug 2026 deadline, urgency evaporates.
   - *Mitigation:* Build recurring value beyond one-time classification (monitoring, updates, re-assessment).

### MEDIUM Risk
4. **Free tools improve** — artificialintelligenceact.eu adds document generation.
   - *Mitigation:* They're a non-profit. Doc generation requires AI infrastructure and ongoing maintenance. Unlikely.

5. **Law firms offer fixed-fee packages** — €500-2000 "EU AI Act compliance pack" from boutique law firms.
   - *Mitigation:* We're faster, cheaper, and available 24/7. Law firms don't scale. Position as "get 80% done instantly, then consult a lawyer for the remaining 20%."

### LOW Risk
6. **Big 4 consultancy creates SMB tool** — Deloitte/KPMG build a self-serve tool.
   - *Mitigation:* They never go downmarket. Their margins depend on billable hours. A €99 tool cannibalizes their consulting revenue.

## 8. Verdict

### The Moat is Real But Time-Limited

**The good news:** There is a genuine, wide gap in the market. Every competitor is enterprise-only with sales-led motions. No one serves SMBs with self-serve EU AI Act compliance. The free tools classify but don't generate compliance artifacts. You are the ONLY product that does classification + docs + action plan at an SMB price point.

**The honest truth:** The classification logic itself is not defensible — it's based on public regulation text. The moat comes from:
1. **Being first** to ship self-serve SMB compliance
2. **The urgency window** (5 months to high-risk deadline)
3. **Accumulated user data & templates** (compound over time)
4. **Brand recognition** as "the SMB EU AI Act tool"

**The risk:** This is a **land grab**. The window between "market realizes SMBs need compliance" and "enterprise players add self-serve tier" is probably 12-18 months. You need to be established with paying customers, SEO rankings, and brand recognition before that window closes.
